The Task Our Machine Solves


Our machine is a mini lift made from a box, designed to raise and lower small objects using only human power. The main problem it solves is how to move items vertically with minimal effort, which is a common challenge in everyday life. Let's think of lifting groceries to a higher shelf or moving books between floors. This model shows how simple machines can make lifting easier and more efficient, even with basic materials.



Mechanical Principles Involved


The main mechanical principle used in our lift is the use of a pulley system. In our design, a piece of straw acts as the axle, and a string(which is a thread) is wound around it. When we turn the handle (which is connected to the straw), the string winds up, pulling the lift platform upwards. This setup uses the principle of mechanical advantage: By turning the handle,we can lift a heavier load with less force than if we tried to pull the platform straight up by hand.

There's also a bit of energy transfer happening. The hand's turning motion (mechanical energy) is transferred through the handle and straw, converting rotational motion into the upward movement of the platform. The design minimizes wasted effort by making the turning smooth and reducing friction as much as possible.

Reflections on Energy Efficiency & User-Centered Design


We tried to make our lift as energy efficient as possible by ensuring the spool turned easily and the string didn't snag. The platform is stable, so objects don't fall off while being lifted. We also made sure everything was safe, no sharp edges, and the lift moves slowly enough that nothing gets damaged.


From a user-centered design perspective, we wanted the lift to be intuitive. Anyone can look at it and understand how to use it. Just turn the handle to go up, and unwind it to go down. We mainly used boxes and thread, making it eco-friendly.

Final Thoughts


Building this lift was a fun way to see physics in action. It's amazing how much easier a simple machine can make a tough job. We learned a lot about mechanical advantages and how important it is to think about the user when designing something, even if it's just made from a box.
